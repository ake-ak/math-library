Overview

This learning path guides you through the fundamental disciplines of mathematics: algebra, geometry, calculus, and statistics. Each domain builds upon foundational concepts and connects to broader mathematical thinking.

You may approach these topics in sequence, or explore specific areas that interest you. Each topic is self-contained and can be studied independently.

Topics

Algebra

The study of abstract mathematical structures and operations. Algebra is foundational to nearly all advanced mathematics, providing tools for solving equations, understanding functions, and manipulating mathematical expressions.

Key Concepts: Equations, polynomials, functions, matrices, abstract structures.

Explore Algebra

Geometry

The study of shapes, spaces, and spatial relationships. Geometry trains the intuition about physical space and provides rigorous methods for reasoning about spatial properties.

Key Concepts: Points, lines, planes, shapes, transformations, spatial reasoning.

Explore Geometry

Calculus

The mathematical study of change and accumulation. Calculus provides tools for analyzing rates of change, areas, volumes, and modeling dynamic systems. It is essential for physics, engineering, and economics.

Key Concepts: Limits, derivatives, integrals, differential equations, series.

Explore Calculus

Statistics

The science of data. Statistics provides methods for collecting, analyzing, and interpreting data to make informed decisions and test hypotheses. It is fundamental to science, economics, and policy.

Key Concepts: Probability, distributions, hypothesis testing, inference, regression.

Explore Statistics

How to Use This Path

  1. Choose a topic that interests you or that you want to understand better. No prior knowledge is assumed.
  2. Follow the lessons in order, taking time to work through examples and problems. Mathematics requires active engagement.
  3. Reference the materials provided in each section. Definitions, theorems, and important formulas are clearly marked.
  4. Ask questions in our Discussion forum if you get stuck or want to explore topics more deeply.
