Contribute

Help build and improve Math Library. We welcome contributions from mathematicians, educators, and learners.

Ways to Contribute

Math Library is built by its community. Here are the main ways you can contribute:

Improve Existing Content

Found a typo, unclear explanation, or error? Help us improve! You can:

  • Suggest clarifications or alternative explanations
  • Report mathematical errors
  • Improve formatting or organization
  • Add examples or worked problems
  • Update references or add new citations

Email your suggestions to Ake.coe.aki@gmail.com or open an issue on GitHub.

Write New Content

Interested in writing for Math Library? We welcome:

  • New lessons or modules for existing courses
  • Entirely new courses or learning paths
  • Blog articles on mathematics, pedagogy, or history
  • Study guides or worked examples
  • Reference materials or tables

Contact us with your proposal at Ake.coe.aki@gmail.com. We'll discuss scope, style, and timeline with you.

Share Resources

Know of excellent books, papers, videos, or resources that should be referenced? Share them with us! We're always looking for quality materials to recommend.

Participate in Discussion

Ask questions, answer others' questions, and share insights in our Discussion forum. Community engagement strengthens everyone's learning.

Report Issues

Found a broken link, confusing explanation, or technical problem? Report it on GitHub or email us. These reports help us maintain quality.

Suggest Features or Topics

Have ideas for new courses, topics, or ways to improve the platform? We'd love to hear them. Discussion about future directions of Math Library is always welcome.

Content Guidelines

If you're contributing written content, please keep these principles in mind:

Clarity First

Write for understanding. Use clear, concrete language. Explain technical terms. Build from first principles. Good mathematics should be readable by educated people, even if they're not experts in the specific area.

Rigor Matters

Be mathematically precise. Definitions should be clear. Claims should be justified. Proofs should be sound. Counterexamples should be correct. We aim for both clarity and accuracy.

Structure for Discovery

Use clear headings, logical organization, and appropriate emphasis. Readers should be able to understand the structure of knowledge at a glance.

Cite Your Sources

Reference where ideas come from. This honors those who came before and helps readers explore further. Include bibliographies for substantial articles.

Permanence in Mind

Write for longevity. Avoid trendy language or examples that will quickly become dated. Focus on enduring principles and timeless truths.

Get in Touch

Ready to contribute? Excellent! Here's how to get started:

  1. Contact us: Email Ake.coe.aki@gmail.com with a brief description of what you'd like to contribute.
  2. Discuss your idea: We'll respond with feedback, discuss scope and style, and align on what would be most helpful.
  3. Create your content: You'll write your contribution, keeping our guidelines in mind.
  4. Review & feedback: We'll review your work, provide constructive feedback, and work with you on refinements.
  5. Integration: Once finalized, we'll integrate your contribution into Math Library.

Attribution & Credit

All contributors are credited for their work. Depending on the contribution, you may receive:

  • Author attribution on the contributed content
  • A profile in our community contributors section
  • Links to your personal site or social media
  • Recognition in announcements about new content

We believe in honoring those who contribute to building this knowledge commons.
