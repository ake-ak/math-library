Algebra

Comprehensive study of algebraic concepts from linear equations to abstract algebraic structures.

← Back to Courses | Math Path
Active Course

Overview

Algebra is the study of abstract mathematical structures and the operations that govern them. This course develops your ability to think symbolically about mathematical relationships, solve equations, and understand the deep connections that underlie mathematics.

Prerequisites

Familiarity with basic arithmetic. No prior algebraic knowledge is assumed.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand and solve linear and quadratic equations
  • Master algebraic manipulation and simplification
  • Work with polynomials and rational functions
  • Understand fundamental properties of abstract algebraic structures
  • Apply algebraic reasoning to problem-solving

Course Modules

Module 1: Foundations of Algebra

Variables, expressions, equations, and the properties that make algebra work. We establish the fundamental rules and conventions that algebraic reasoning depends on.

  • Variables and expressions
  • Equations and equivalence
  • Properties of equality and operations
  • Solving linear equations

Module 2: Linear Systems and Functions

Understanding functions as mathematical relationships. Linear functions and systems of equations are fundamental to modeling and understanding change.

  • Functions and function notation
  • Linear functions and lines
  • Systems of linear equations
  • Applications and modeling

Module 3: Polynomial and Rational Functions

Understanding more complex algebraic functions and their behavior. Polynomials are essential structures in algebra and calculus.

  • Polynomial operations and factoring
  • Quadratic equations and functions
  • Polynomial division and remainder theorem
  • Rational functions and asymptotes

Module 4: Exponential and Logarithmic Functions

Functions that model growth, decay, and change over time. Essential for applications in science, finance, and engineering.

  • Exponential functions
  • Logarithmic functions and properties
  • Solving exponential and logarithmic equations
  • Applications to real-world phenomena

Module 5: Introduction to Abstract Algebra

Moving beyond concrete numbers to abstract algebraic structures. This module introduces groups, rings, and fields—fundamental objects in modern mathematics.

  • Groups and group operations
  • Rings and fields
  • Vector spaces (introduction)
  • Connections to other areas of mathematics

References

Recommended books and resources for deeper study:

  • Van der Waerden - Algebra (comprehensive reference)
  • Dummit and Foote - Abstract Algebra (modern treatment)
  • Stillwell - Mathematics and Its History (historical perspective)
