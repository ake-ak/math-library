Calculus

The mathematical study of change: limits, derivatives, integrals, and infinite series.

‚Üê Back to Courses | Math Path
Active Course

Overview

Calculus is the mathematics of change and motion. It provides powerful tools for analyzing rates of change (derivatives) and accumulation (integrals). Calculus is essential to physics, engineering, economics, and nearly all applied sciences.

Prerequisites

Solid understanding of functions and algebra. The Algebra course or equivalent knowledge is recommended.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand limits and continuity
  • Master differentiation and its applications
  • Master integration and its applications
  • Understand the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus
  • Apply calculus to modeling and problem-solving
  • Understand convergence of infinite series

Course Modules

Module 1: Limits and Continuity

The foundation of calculus. Understanding what it means for a function to approach a value, and how continuity governs the behavior of functions.

  • Informal and formal definitions of limits
  • Laws of limits
  • Continuity and discontinuity
  • The Intermediate Value Theorem

Module 2: Differentiation

The derivative captures the rate of change of a function. This module develops the concept and techniques of differentiation, fundamental to all of calculus.

  • The derivative as a limit
  • Differentiation rules (power, product, quotient, chain)
  • Derivatives of transcendental functions
  • Implicit differentiation and related rates

Module 3: Applications of Differentiation

Using derivatives to understand and optimize functions, analyze curves, and model real-world phenomena.

  • Optimization (maxima and minima)
  • Curve sketching and behavior analysis
  • Linear approximation and differentials
  • Newton's method for solving equations

Module 4: Integration

Integration is the inverse of differentiation, but also the tool for computing areas and accumulation. This module develops both the concept and techniques.

  • Antiderivatives and indefinite integrals
  • The definite integral and Riemann sums
  • The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus
  • Integration techniques (substitution, by parts, partial fractions)

Module 5: Applications of Integration

Using integrals to compute areas, volumes, work, and model accumulation processes.

  • Areas between curves
  • Volumes of solids of revolution
  • Arc length and surface area
  • Work, force, and physical applications

Module 6: Differential Equations and Series

Introduction to differential equations (equations involving derivatives) and infinite series, extending calculus to more advanced domains.

  • Separable and linear differential equations
  • Sequences and series
  • Convergence tests
  • Power series and Taylor series
