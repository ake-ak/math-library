Geometry

The study of shapes, spaces, and spatial relationships in mathematics.

← Back to Courses | Math Path
Active Course

Overview

Geometry is the ancient and essential branch of mathematics concerned with shapes, spaces, and their properties. From the elegant Euclidean geometry of ancient Greece to modern non-Euclidean geometries and topology, this course develops spatial intuition and rigorous geometric reasoning.

Prerequisites

Basic understanding of algebra is helpful but not required. No prior geometry knowledge is assumed.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand Euclidean geometry and classical theorems
  • Develop spatial reasoning and visualization
  • Master geometric proofs and constructions
  • Understand transformations and symmetry
  • Explore non-Euclidean geometries
  • Apply geometry to real-world problems

Course Modules

Module 1: Foundational Concepts

Points, lines, angles, and planes—the basic building blocks of geometry. We establish the fundamental definitions and axioms that Euclidean geometry rests upon.

  • Fundamental objects and definitions
  • Angles and angle relationships
  • Parallel lines and transversals
  • Postulates and axioms

Module 2: Triangles and Congruence

Triangles are the simplest polygons but contain remarkable properties. This module explores congruence, similarity, and the rich structure of triangle geometry.

  • Triangle classifications and properties
  • Congruence theorems (SSS, SAS, ASA)
  • Similarity and proportionality
  • Special points and lines in triangles

Module 3: Polygons and Circles

From quadrilaterals to regular polygons, and the special properties of circles. We explore the connections between these fundamental shapes.

  • Properties of quadrilaterals
  • Regular polygons
  • Circle theorems and inscribed angles
  • Tangent lines and power of a point

Module 4: Area, Perimeter, and Volume

Computing measurements of geometric objects. From 2D areas to 3D volumes, we develop formulas and understand their geometric meaning.

  • Area formulas for polygons and circles
  • Perimeter and arc length
  • Volume of solids
  • Surface area

Module 5: Transformations and Symmetry

Geometric transformations—rotations, reflections, translations, and dilations—reveal the hidden symmetries in shapes.

  • Isometries and congruence
  • Similarity transformations
  • Symmetries and groups
  • Fractal geometry

Module 6: Coordinate Geometry and Beyond

Using algebra to study geometry, and exploring the extensions of Euclidean geometry to non-Euclidean spaces.

  • Analytic geometry and coordinates
  • Conic sections
  • Non-Euclidean geometries
  • Introduction to topology

References

Recommended books and resources:

  • Euclid - Elements (the classical foundation)
  • Brannan, Esplen, Gray - Geometry (comprehensive modern text)
  • Hartshorne - Geometry: Euclid and Beyond (rigorous treatment)
